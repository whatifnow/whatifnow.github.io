What we're building

This is everything What If Now is building, shipping, considering, and has decided not to build. Tap any leaf for the detail behind the decision.

  1. Done

    • Right to Stay

      righttostay.ie tells any Irish tenant their rights under current housing law — free, in plain language.

    • Tenant case-pack handover

      When a tenant consents, righttostay.ie compiles and forwards a structured case-pack to a Stare-licensed firm.

  2. In Progress

    • Stare

      Stare indexes every published decision from Ireland's housing tribunal — semantically searchable, with outcome statistics per fact pattern.

    • Search by matter type

      Narrow a search to a specific dispute type and surface decisions grouped by category and outcome.

    • AI analysis of precedents

      Each indexed decision carries an AI-generated outcome summary with every source named and linked.

  3. Planned

    • Data rights workspace

      Manages the full lifecycle of a GDPR subject access request, from draft to delivery and response tracking.

    • AI risk checker

      Classifies an AI system against EU AI Act risk tiers and surfaces the applicable compliance obligations.

    • AI Act compliance templates

      Pre-structured documentation templates for the EU AI Act's required technical file, risk register, and conformity declaration.

  4. Exploring

    • Stare for more practice areas

      We are considering whether Stare's indexing model extends to employment, commercial, or constitutional precedent.

    • iOS and Android apps

      Whether righttostay.ie warrants dedicated native apps for iOS and Android is under active evaluation.

    • AI compliance platform

      We are exploring a fuller compliance platform built around the EU AI Act; scope and sequence are not yet set.

    • Right to Stay for AI tools

      We are exploring a free, public tool interface so AI assistants can query righttostay.ie's housing-law guidance.

  5. Pruned

    • AI-ranked solicitor matching

      We considered routing tenants to solicitors via an AI-ranked shortlist; the Solicitors Acts prohibit evaluating practitioners by suitability.

    • Commission-based referral fees

      We considered charging solicitors a per-referral commission; the Solicitors Acts prohibit fee-sharing of that kind, so we stopped.
